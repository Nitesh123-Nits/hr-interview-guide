Hiring Process

The Hiring Journey

Understand every stage of the process so you know exactly what to expect and how to prepare for each milestone.

Step 01
Application & Resume Screening
Your resume is reviewed by ATS and recruiters. Tailor keywords to the job description, quantify achievements, and keep formatting clean. This is where first impressions begin — before any conversation.
Step 02
HR Screening Call (15–30 mins)
A preliminary call to validate your background, salary expectations, and availability. Be concise, professional, and enthusiastic. Have your resume, a notepad, and questions ready. This decides if you move forward.
Step 03
Technical / Domain Rounds
Varies by role — coding challenges, case studies, assignments, or domain Q&A panels. Focus on structured thinking and communication alongside technical skill. Even here, soft skills are observed.
Step 04
HR Final Round
Deep dive into your personality, culture fit, past experiences, and motivations. STAR-structured answers are essential. This is where behavioural and situational questions dominate — preparation is everything.
Step 05
Offer Discussion & Negotiation
You'll receive a verbal or written offer. This is your window to negotiate — salary, joining date, benefits. Be polite, confident, and data-driven. A well-negotiated offer can add significant value over your career.

Frameworks

Communication Frameworks

Structured approaches to ensure your answers are clear, compelling, and memorable.

STAR Method
The gold standard for behavioural questions. Keeps your answers focused and evidence-driven.
SDescribe the Situation briefly
TExplain the Task you were responsible for
ADetail the Action you specifically took
RShare the measurable Result
Clear & Concise Speaking
Avoid rambling. Structure your thoughts before speaking using the Pyramid Principle.
1Lead with your main point first
2Support with 2–3 evidence points
3Wrap with a brief conclusion
4Pause — don't fill silence with filler words
Body Language Tips
55% of communication is non-verbal. What you don't say matters as much as what you do.
→Maintain natural, steady eye contact
→Sit upright, lean slightly forward
→Nod to show active listening
→Keep hands visible and still
→Smile genuinely — warmth builds rapport
Tone & Confidence
Your vocal delivery shapes how your answers are perceived. Sound confident without being arrogant.
→Speak at a measured, steady pace
→Vary pitch to show enthusiasm
→Avoid upward inflection (sounds unsure)
→Replace "um/uh" with a brief pause
→Record yourself practising — review honestly
What Not To Do

Common HR Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls that cost candidates the job — even when they're technically qualified.

Over-Explaining & Rambling
❌ Don't say
"Well, it started when I was in college and then my first job, and there was this project, and then basically what happened was… where was I?"
✓ Instead
"I have 4 years in [field], most recently at [Company], where I led [specific achievement]. I'm now looking for [role] because [clear reason]."
Badmouthing Previous Employer
❌ Don't say
"My last manager was terrible and the team was completely dysfunctional. I couldn't wait to leave."
✓ Instead
"I gained a lot from that role, but I'm looking for an environment with more [growth opportunities / larger scale / better mentorship] to align with my career goals."
Being Too Generic
❌ Don't say
"I'm a hard worker, very passionate, and a team player. I just love working with people and solving problems."
✓ Instead
"At [Company], I reduced onboarding time by 40% by automating a manual process — that's the kind of problem I enjoy untangling."
Discussing Salary Too Early
❌ Don't say
"What's the salary for this role? I need at least ₹X per month or it's a dealbreaker for me."
✓ Instead
"I'm flexible and would love to understand the full scope of the role first. Happy to share my expectations once I have more context."
No Questions for the Interviewer
❌ Don't say
"No, I think I have everything I need. I'm good. Thanks."
✓ Instead
"What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?" or "What do you love most about working here?"
Lying or Exaggerating
❌ Don't say
"Yes, I led a team of 20 engineers" — when you informally mentored 2 people on a project.
✓ Instead
"I mentored two junior engineers during that project and coordinated cross-functionally with a team of 8." — specific and honest.
Strategy

HR Round Strategy

A structured plan for before, during, and after your HR interview to maximise your chances of success.

Before the Interview
01
Research the Company DeeplyKnow their mission, recent news, product, culture, and competitors. Reference this naturally in answers.
02
Re-read the Job DescriptionMap your experience to every key requirement. Prepare examples for each.
03
Prepare 5–7 STAR StoriesCover leadership, conflict, failure, success, collaboration. Reuse them for different questions.
04
Know Your NumbersCurrent CTC, expected CTC, notice period, years of experience. Have these ready, not approximate.
05
Prepare 5 Thoughtful QuestionsQuestions show engagement. Prepare about team culture, growth path, challenges in the role.
During the Interview
01
Start with a Strong IntroductionYour first 90 seconds set the tone. Practice your "Tell me about yourself" until it's natural.
02
Take 2–3 Seconds Before AnsweringPausing to think signals thoughtfulness. Never rush into a rambling answer.
03
Mirror the Interviewer's EnergyMatch their conversational pace — don't be too formal if they're casual, or too casual if they're formal.
04
Bridge to Your StrengthsSteer every answer toward your core narrative: who you are, what you bring, why this role.
05
Show Genuine CuriosityReact to what they share. Ask clarifying follow-ups. It shows you're listening, not just reciting.
After the Interview
01
Send a Thank-You Email Within 24 HoursReference something specific from your conversation. It reinforces your interest and sets you apart.
02
Follow Up Politely If No ResponseWait 5–7 business days, then send a brief, courteous follow-up. Don't chase more than twice.
03
Reflect and DocumentWrite down questions asked and your answers. Identify what worked and what to improve for next rounds.
04
Don't Put All Eggs in One BasketKeep interviewing until you have a signed offer. Stay engaged in your process but don't stall others.
Negotiation

Salary Negotiation Guide

Negotiation is expected. Use these scripts and strategies to secure the best possible offer without jeopardising the relationship.

Sample Scripts

Scenario 1 — When Asked for Expectations

Use this when the recruiter asks first.

"Based on my research into market rates for this role and location, and given my [X years of experience / specific skill], I'm looking at a range of ₹X–₹Y. That said, I'm open to discussing the full compensation package including benefits and growth opportunities."
Scenario 2 — Counter-Offering

After receiving an offer lower than expected.

"Thank you so much for the offer — I'm genuinely excited about this role and the team. Based on my current compensation and market benchmarks, I was hoping we could get closer to ₹X. Is there flexibility there?"
Scenario 3 — Asking for Time

When you need time to evaluate the offer.

"I really appreciate the offer. Could I have until [specific date — 48–72 hrs] to review the full details? I want to give it the thoughtful consideration it deserves before committing."
Do's
  • Research market rates on Glassdoor, LinkedIn, AmbitionBox before negotiating
  • Negotiate salary and other benefits (WFH, bonus, learning budget, ESOPs)
  • Always get the offer in writing before resigning
  • Stay warm and collaborative throughout — it's a conversation, not a battle
  • Know your BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)
  • Negotiate joining date if you need more time
Don'ts
  • Give your current salary as a starting anchor too early
  • Negotiate over WhatsApp or chat — always do it on call or email
  • Make ultimatums or threats
  • Accept or decline immediately under pressure
  • Reveal how desperate you are for the job
  • Negotiate more than twice on the same item

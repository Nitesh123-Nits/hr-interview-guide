Why It Matters

Silence is the worst answer.

Saying "No, I'm good" signals disinterest, lack of preparation, and missed opportunity.

⚡

Shows Genuine Interest

Thoughtful questions prove you've researched the role, team, and company — not just applied to every posting.

🧭

Reveals Your Priorities

What you ask signals your values. Architecture questions show depth. Culture questions show self-awareness.

🎯

Differentiates You

Most candidates ask generic questions. Sharp, specific questions make you memorable long after the call ends.

🔍

Helps You Evaluate Them

You're interviewing them too. Great questions uncover red flags, culture fit, and growth potential.

High-Impact Questions

Ask these.
Be remembered.

No questions match your search.
🎯Role & Expectations
What does success look like in this role at 30, 60, and 90 days?
Why it works: Forces clarity on expectations. If they can't answer, that's a red flag.
Best asked to: Hiring manager
How did the team arrive at the current priorities for this role?
Why it works: Reveals decision-making culture and whether this role has a clear mandate.
Best asked to: Team lead / EM
What are the biggest challenges someone in this role would face in the first six months?
Why it works: Shows maturity. You're not pretending it'll be easy — you want to be prepared.
Best asked to: Hiring manager
Is this a new role or a backfill? What happened to the previous person?
Why it works: Uncovers the real story. High turnover or vague answers are warning signs.
Best asked to: Recruiter / EM
🏗System Design & Architecture
What does your current architecture look like at a high level? Are there known pain points you're actively addressing?
Why it works: Engineers love talking architecture. Reveals technical debt awareness and honesty.
Best asked to: Senior engineer / Tech lead
How do you make decisions about adopting new technologies vs. standardizing on existing ones?
Why it works: Surfaces maturity around tech selection and whether it's driven by hype or pragmatism.
Best asked to: Principal / Staff engineer
Where are the scalability bottlenecks in your system today, and how are you tackling them?
Why it works: Shows you think at scale. The answer also tells you what you'd be working on.
Best asked to: Senior engineer
How do you handle data consistency across distributed services?
Why it works: Tests the team's distributed systems depth. Vague answers = potential chaos.
Best asked to: Tech lead / Architect
⚙️Engineering Practices
What does your code review process look like? How long do PRs typically sit before review?
Why it works: PR velocity is a proxy for team collaboration health. Long queues = bottlenecks or siloing.
Best asked to: Engineer on the team
How do you manage technical debt? Is there dedicated time for it each sprint?
Why it works: Teams that ignore tech debt move fast — until they don't. Good signal of engineering maturity.
Best asked to: EM / Senior engineer
What does your testing philosophy look like? What's your coverage culture?
Why it works: Testing culture predicts code quality, on-call burden, and release confidence.
Best asked to: Any engineer
How often do you ship to production? What does your CI/CD pipeline look like?
Why it works: Deployment frequency is a DORA key metric. Reveals how agile the team actually is.
Best asked to: DevOps / Senior engineer
Do engineers write RFCs or ADRs before major changes? How are technical decisions documented?
Why it works: Shows you value documentation and async collaboration — huge signal of seniority.
Best asked to: Tech lead
🔥Production & Debugging
What does your on-call rotation look like? How many incidents happen per month on average?
Why it works: On-call burden directly affects your quality of life. High incident rate = instability.
Best asked to: EM / Engineer
Can you walk me through a recent production incident and how your team handled it?
Why it works: How a team handles incidents reveals blameless culture vs. blame culture instantly.
Best asked to: Senior engineer / EM
What observability stack do you use? How do you monitor system health?
Why it works: Metrics, logs, traces. If they have none, production is a mystery box.
Best asked to: DevOps / SRE / Engineer
What's your post-mortem process after incidents? Is it blameless?
Why it works: Blameless culture = psychological safety. The answer reveals team maturity.
Best asked to: EM / Tech lead
🚀Growth & Ownership
How does the engineering leveling and promotion process work here?
Why it works: Vague answers signal opaque politics. Clear rubrics = fair growth.
Best asked to: EM / HR
What's the biggest project this team could hand me ownership of in 12 months?
Why it works: Shows ambition and long-term thinking. Interviewers remember this.
Best asked to: Hiring manager
Do engineers here get time for side projects, open-source, or conference talks?
Why it works: Signals you care about craft beyond the job. Also reveals how the company invests in engineers.
Best asked to: Any engineer
What have been the career trajectories of engineers who joined at my level in the past 2–3 years?
Why it works: Real data beats promises. If they can't name examples, that's telling.
Best asked to: EM / Recruiter
🤝Team & Culture
What does the team's communication style look like — async-first or heavy on meetings?
Why it works: Meeting culture directly affects your deep work time and sanity.
Best asked to: Any team member
How does the team handle disagreements on technical direction?
Why it works: Reveals psychological safety and conflict resolution maturity. No disagreements = echo chamber.
Best asked to: Engineer / Tech lead
What's one thing you genuinely wish was different about working here?
Why it works: Disarms corporate filters. You'll get authentic, candid answers. Most memorable question you can ask.
Best asked to: Engineer (not EM)
How does your team celebrate wins? How do you handle failure?
Why it works: Culture is revealed at extremes — how people act after a big win and a tough loss.
Best asked to: Team member
If you could change one thing about how this team operates, what would it be?
Why it works: A variant on the candid question. Great for panel interviews with multiple engineers.
Best asked to: Engineer
What Not to Ask

Questions that hurt you.

These signal entitlement, lack of research, or disinterest. Avoid them completely.

❌
"So… what does your company do?"

You should have researched this before applying. Asking this shows you didn't bother.

❌
"How many vacation days do I get?"

Save comp & benefits for an offer stage. Asking too early signals you care more about time off than the role.

❌
"Do I have to work weekends?"

Immediately raises concerns about your work ethic and commitment, even if the concern is valid.

❌
"Will I get to work on AI/blockchain/etc.?"

Tech-specific FOMO questions suggest you're chasing hype, not solving real problems.

❌
"When will I hear back?" (as your only question)

Using your question slot only to ask about the process signals you have no real curiosity about the role.

❌
"No, I think I'm good!"

The worst possible answer. Even one thoughtful question is infinitely better than silence.

Smart Combos

Questions that pair perfectly.

Use these 2–3 question sequences to create a memorable, flowing conversation.

Combo 1 — The Architecture Deep Dive
1 "What does your current architecture look like at a high level?"
→
2 "Where are the known scalability bottlenecks, and how are you addressing them?"
→
3 "How do you make decisions about adopting new technologies?"
This sequence creates a natural conversation flow. You'll get a whiteboard architecture session in a Q&A format — and interviewers love it.
Combo 2 — The Culture Litmus Test
1 "Can you walk me through a recent production incident?"
→
2 "What was your post-mortem process? Was it blameless?"
→
3 "What's one thing you wish was different about working here?"
A masterclass in cultural due diligence. Moves from technical to personal. The last question always yields the most honest answer.
Combo 3 — The Growth Trajectory
1 "What does success look like in this role at 30, 60, and 90 days?"
→
2 "How does the engineering leveling and promotion process work?"
→
3 "What's the biggest project I could own in 12 months?"
Shows ambition + planning. You're not just asking about the job — you're already thinking about impact. This combo makes you memorable.
Pro Tips

Advanced tactics for elite candidates.

01

Tailor questions to the interviewer's role

A CEO cares about vision. An engineer cares about code quality. A recruiter knows culture. Match the question to the person.

Example conversation:
Interviewer (EM): "Any questions for me?"
You: "How does your team's on-call burden compare to a year ago — is it improving?"
Interviewer: [pauses, leans in] "Actually, that's something we've been actively working on…"
02

Ask one question at a time — listen actively

Don't machine-gun 5 questions at once. Ask one, listen, follow up naturally. It becomes a real conversation, not an interrogation.

03

Reference something from the interview

If they mentioned a challenge earlier, ask about it: "You mentioned you're rebuilding the payments service — is that something I'd be involved in?" Shows you were listening.

04

Prepare 6–8 questions, expect to ask 2–3

Some will get answered during the interview itself. Having extras ensures you're never scrambling for something to say.

05

Note their answers — use them in your thank-you email

Reference a specific answer in your follow-up: "Your point about blameless post-mortems really resonated with me." It's a powerful closing move.
